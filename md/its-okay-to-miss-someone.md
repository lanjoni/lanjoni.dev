# It's okay to miss someone

I'm not the best person to express my feelings. I learned to live in a way that avoided showing feelings in public because it seemed wrong, it seemed ugly, it even seemed like people would judge you for it. Whenever I felt something, I kept it to myself and dealt with it alone, without exposing myself.

Dealing with it can often be seen in many ways, whether it's taking it out on something or doing something. I stayed alone, and often, I didn't let myself feel. I blocked myself from feeling the "things" coming and I blocked myself from having sensations that every human being should have someday.

In recent years it has been difficult to deal with sadness, to deal with loss, with longing. It has been very difficult to deal with change, to deal with new responsibilities and, more than anything, to always seem okay.

I lost people I cared about a lot, I lost experiences that could have happened, I lost moments that I could have let all of that flow.

I lost a friend I cared about. I lost a mother who did everything for me until her last breath. I lost a partner who always knew how to love me in so many different ways, and even if it was a simple love, it was a pure love.

I feel that life has been very quick in taking people from me. I feel that life has been somewhat strange when it comes to change. I feel that life has led me to do things that I myself wouldn't agree with. But I think that's what life is about, or so it seems.

I know that we need to be strong, we need to understand that everything in life is constantly changing, we need to accept it, but I would like to look and be able to change some things.

I know that I can't give my friend one last hug. I know that I can't spend an afternoon talking with my other mother. I know that I can't show affection to my partner one more time. And I also know that the only thing I can have of them today is the memory of all the happy moments we had together. Of all the good things they were able to leave me and all the lessons they were able to teach me.

In Portuguese, the word "saudade" means when we miss something or someone, having a profound meaning in the sense of not being able to have it now. For me, "saudade" is marked by the three of you, who made my life better, and I know you did everything for me. Thank you for fighting, thank you for living, and more than anything, thank you for giving me the privilege of knowing you.

Therefore, it's okay to feel all of this, and more importantly, to let the feelings flow. That's *what* makes us *human*.

A special thank you to my partner, Rebeca, who was with me through all moments, happy or sad, always being loved. Thank you for existing and giving me the privilege of knowing you, I loved you so much, and today I hope you can rest. I will always remember you.
